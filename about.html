Robotics Engineering Intern
An MRI room is hostile to most electronics, no ferrous metal, no stray magnetic interference, and every actuator has to hit a torque-speed-power target without the usual tools engineers reach for. I modeled the actuators in Python and ran 500+ parameter sweeps, eventually landing a 15% efficiency gain over baseline. Midway through, integration just stopped working, a USB HID vs. UART conflict was silently dropping communication between components. No error message pointed at it directly, I had to trace the signal path manually to find it. Switched to direct UART and the blocker disappeared. If I'm being evaluated on debugging under ambiguity, this is the project where the problem wasn't in the code I wrote, it was in how two systems were talking to each other.

Undergraduate Researcher
An AR sandbox needs to know the physical shape of the sand in real time and react to it, which means stitching together sensor data, depth cameras, and a microcontroller network that all talk to each other without lag. 2021
Government of India
Water Ambassador, Gurugram
A furniture factory in India was running its handwashing taps continuously, with no automatic shutoff, wasting enormous volumes of water every month with nobody tracking it. The hard part wasn't the sensor logic, it was getting the timing right so the tap felt natural to use, not like fighting a machine. I built Dripmise, an automatic timer tap that cuts water during the soaping interval and resumes for a fixed rinse window. Installed at Timber Taste, it measured out to 96,000 litres saved per month. The Government of India gave it an INR 10,000 award for water conservation and appointed me Water Ambassador for Gurugram. This is the project that proved hardware doesn't need to be complicated to matter, it just needs to solve a real problem at a real site.
