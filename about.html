Promaxo Inc. · Oakland, CA
Robotics Engineering Intern
Promaxo's MRI-compatible robotic system uses a pneumatic stepper motor, PneuStep, and the question going in was how different design and operating parameters affect its torque, rotational speed, and overall performance before any physical prototype gets built. Under my manager's guidance, I built simulation models in MATLAB and Python, then ran parametric experiments across multiple design variables to see how each one shifted torque, speed, and performance trends. The work was simulation-based only, no physical hardware was built during the internship. I analyzed the resulting data, compared outputs to identify optimal operating regions, and produced performance plots to support future motor optimization decisions.

LIDAR Lab · Georgia Tech
Electrical Engineer
A robotics team needed two high-torque servos controlled over USB from an onboard Linux computer, and the board they had been recommended turned out to be wrong for the job, it spoke UART, not the PWM signal the servos actually needed. I diagnosed the mismatch and pointed the team toward a USB-to-PWM controller instead. 2021
Government of India
Water Ambassador, Gurugram
A furniture factory in India was running its handwashing taps continuously, with no automatic shutoff, wasting enormous volumes of water every month with nobody tracking it. The hard part wasn't the sensor logic, it was getting the timing right so the tap felt natural to use, not like fighting a machine. I built Dripmise, an automatic timer tap that cuts water during the soaping interval and resumes for a fixed rinse window. Installed at Timber Taste, it measured out to 96,000 litres saved per month. The Ministry of Water Resources, Government of India, gave it an INR 10,000 award for water conservation and appointed me Water Ambassador for Gurugram.
